About me
Arnold Diaz

I am a medical student at Northeast Ohio Medical University in Akron, Ohio, currently in my third year. I've always cared deeply about community, whether that means helping preserve cultural heritage for Latino families or supporting women and children navigating tough situations. Much of that commitment has taken shape through hands-on work like the St. Bernard Health Fair, where I helped provide screenings, health education, and resources to underserved community members. Through the Latino Medical Student Association (LMSA), I've found a platform to advocate for health equity, collaborate with peers dedicated to culturally competent care, and work to bridge the gap between medical institutions and the Latino families they serve. These experiences have deepened my understanding of the barriers many communities face and continue to fuel my drive to be a physician who leads with both clinical skill and cultural awareness.

Outside of school and volunteering, I enjoy hiking, reading, playing my guitar, and exercising. I believe that even small acts of service can ripple outward and create real change in people's lives.

Proyecto RAÍCES

Akron, OH · Nonprofit

Proyecto RAÍCES provides educational and social support to children of Latino/Hispanic families in the Akron area, helping them thrive while maintaining their cultural heritage. As a facilitator of the bi-monthly "Sábado" Program, I helped create a space where Latino and Hispanic children could learn, play, and celebrate who they are. From bilingual story time and hands-on arts and crafts to STEM workshops and healthy lifestyle activities, every session was designed to spark curiosity while keeping cultural roots alive. One of the most rewarding parts was presenting cultural programs that brought families together and strengthened community pride. Building real relationships with the children and their families keeping them informed, involved, and connected made this work deeply meaningful to me.

Women's & Children's Shelter

ACCESS · Akron, OH

I'm passionate about supporting families in crisis. Healthcare Screening Initiative

St. Herman's House · Cleveland, OH

A hands-on healthcare project providing free blood pressure screenings to residents at St. Herman's House homeless shelter in Cleveland. Our goal is to identify individuals with elevated blood pressure and connect them with free medical clinics in the area.

Project Roadmap

1

Volunteer & Build Relationships

Begin by volunteering regularly at St. Herman's House — helping with meals and getting to know the residents and staff. A guardian must be present for volunteers under 18.

2

Plan the Screening

After about a month, coordinate with shelter management to schedule a time and day for healthcare screenings. Purchase an automatic blood pressure cuff and prepare a printed list of free medical clinics in Cleveland.

3

Conduct Screenings

Offer free blood pressure screenings to shelter residents. For anyone with elevated readings, provide printed information about free clinics where they can receive follow-up care.

4

Track & Grow

Maintain a log of screenings performed and abnormal readings identified. Repeat every one to two months and expand the project's reach and recognition over time.
